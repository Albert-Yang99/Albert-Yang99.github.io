About me

My interest in economics goes back to my high school days when I read extensively about business cycles and inflation. With China being “a Keynesian country”, the public sector plays a profound role in its economy, so I chose public finance as my undergraduate major. And through the study of macroeconomics during my sophomore year, I found my sincere research interest, and I resolved to lead a life in academia. My undergraduate degree at Zhongnan University of Economics and Law was quite robust, and I laid a solid foundation in the areas of economics and public finance. I think it's fair to say that even in my freshman year, I was looking towards doing a Ph.D. in some areas of economics. With that in mind, I signed up for modules such as Calculus, Microeconomics, Macroeconomics, and Principles of Public Finance, whilst I also learned Machine Learning and Python beyond my structured classroom study. .
